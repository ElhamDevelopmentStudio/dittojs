# Governance

DittoJs uses a maintainer-led, contribution-driven governance model. The model is intentionally lightweight while the project is pre-1.0 and should evolve as sustained contributors emerge.

## Principles

- Technical decisions follow the documented product and architecture contracts.
- User safety, generated-project correctness, and maintainability outrank feature volume.
- Decisions should be visible in issues, pull requests, specifications, or architecture records.
- Authority grows through consistent, constructive contribution and stewardship.
- No contributor is entitled to review, merge, or release access solely by volume of code.

## Roles

### Contributors

Anyone who participates through code, documentation, design, issue triage, testing, or community support. Contributors follow the contribution guide and Code of Conduct.

### Reviewers

Trusted contributors who have demonstrated project understanding and may provide authoritative reviews in defined areas. Review approval does not itself grant merge access.

### Maintainers

Maintainers triage issues, review and merge changes, manage releases, moderate community spaces, and protect project architecture and security. Maintainers are expected to remain responsive, disclose relevant conflicts of interest, and recuse themselves when appropriate.

### Project lead

The project lead has final responsibility for product direction, maintainer appointments, security response, and resolving decisions that do not reach consensus. The current project lead is [@ElhamDevelopmentStudio](https://github.com/ElhamDevelopmentStudio).

## Decision making

Routine changes use lazy consensus through issue and pull-request review. Significant changes should begin with a proposal describing the problem, constraints, compatibility impact, alternatives, migration, and tests.

Consensus is preferred. When consensus cannot be reached in a reasonable time, the project lead records a decision and its rationale. Security incidents may be handled privately until coordinated disclosure is safe.

## Becoming a reviewer or maintainer

Appointments consider:

- Sustained, high-quality contributions over time.
- Sound judgment about scope, compatibility, testing, and user impact.
- Respectful review and community conduct.
- Reliability in issue triage or maintenance work.
- Understanding of the resolver-first architecture.

Existing maintainers nominate and approve new reviewers or maintainers. Roles may be scoped to specific packages or domains.

## Inactivity and removal

Maintainers may step down at any time. Access may be removed after prolonged inactivity, repeated failure to follow project policies, security risk, or Code of Conduct enforcement. Removal decisions should be documented when privacy and safety permit.

## Changes to governance

Governance changes require a public pull request and explicit project-lead approval. Material changes should allow community review before merging.
